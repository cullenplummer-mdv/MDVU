Slide 7 of 12 — Environmental Mandates
Installation Requirements

Environmental Mandates

🛡️
IP65 Weather Rating

You must purchase and use the official MyDoorView backbox to obtain the IP65 weather resistance rating. The backbox is not optional — it is a warranty requirement.

🔩
Supported Mount Types

Three certified mounting configurations are supported: Flush Mount, Surface Mount, and Surface Mount with Hood. Use only approved mounts to maintain warranty.

🌡️
Operating Temperature Range

The VUE Series is rated for −40°F to 140°F. Installations in extreme climates should include appropriate shading or ventilation to stay within this range.

🧹
Screen & Maintenance

Clean the touch display with a soft cloth only. Never use chemicals, irrigation spray, or pressure washers — these permanently damage the screen coating and void the warranty.

☀️
Sunlight Warning: Avoid direct South-facing installs. Overheating causes screen de-lamination which is classified as environmental damage — not covered by warranty. Use a hood or shading solution where needed.

Slide 8 of 12 — Power Requirements & Safety Rules
Critical Mandates

Power Requirements & Safety Rules

⚡ Power Sources

  • PoE+ (D Port): Must use a PoE+ type power supply for network-powered installations.
  • 12V DC (C Port): Recommended for wire runs exceeding standard PoE distance limits.
  • Minimum Amperage: 12V DC installations require a minimum 3-amp power supply.

📏 Wiring Standards

  • Minimum Gauge: All wiring must be at least 16 AWG.
  • Lock Power Upgrade (A1 Port): If powering an external lock from A1, upgrade the power supply to a minimum of 5 amps.
  • Max Runs: Cat5 PoE+ max 40ft · Cat6 PoE+ max 200ft.
⚠️
The PoE Safety Rule: Lock power shall not be used when the panel is powered via PoE. If powering an external lock, switch to 12V DC and upgrade to a 5-amp supply.

Slide 06 — Critical Integration Wiring
Wiegand & The Relay 2 Safety Net

Critical Integration Wiring

📟

VUE-7R Integration

Utilizes an embedded slot for a partner's physical mullion reader. Data is passed via the A-Port bank (A6–A10) to the partner controller.

A-Port: A6–A10
🔌

VUE-5ER Integration

Uses a built-in Wavelynx APEX Reader. All wiring is handled via a 9-wire pigtail, which includes 12V, OSDP, and Relay connections.

9-Wire Pigtail
📲

VUE-QR Integration

A non-electronic placard requiring no wiring or power. Triggers access entirely through a Cloud-to-Cloud API handshake between MDV and partner.

Cloud-to-Cloud Only
🛡️

The "Safety Net" — Relay 2

Wire Relay 2 (B4–B6 on VUE-7R) directly to the lock. Ensures residents can still enter via local credentials if the internet or API link fails.

Relay 2: Offline Failover
💡
Pro Tip: Always wire Relay 2 as a local fail-over on every integration job. This single step is what separates a good install from a great one — it guarantees resident access survives any network or cloud outage.

Slide 07 — Troubleshooting & Diagnostic Logic
Diagnostic Logic

Troubleshooting Integration Issues

🚫
Access Denied — No Event Log

If a credential is denied at the panel but no event appears in the MDV log, the issue is internal to the Partner Software. Check for expired users or incorrect permission levels in Brivo/PDK.

🔒
Access Granted — Door Stays Locked

If the log shows "Access Granted" and the relay clicks but the door remains locked, the issue is physical wiring between the relay (or partner controller) and the lock hardware.

🌐
Video Fails / Black Screen

If remote unlock works but video calls show a black screen, the local firewall is blocking high-bandwidth video ports. The D-Port requires no inbound forwarding, but outbound video traffic must be allowed.

📍
VUE-QR "Away from Door" Error

This indicates a geo-fencing failure. The user must enable precise location services on their device to verify they are physically at the entrance when scanning the placard.
